OBJETIVES

Students should be trained to solve problems that have not yet been raised, for professions that do not yet exist and to deal with technologies that have not yet been invented.

As the Digital Library Task Force  points out in the current era, literacy means, in addition:

- To be able to use diverse technologies of adequate and effective way to search and to recover information, to interpret the results of search, and to judge the quality of the recovered information;

- Understand the relationships between technology, lifelong learning and proper information management;

- Use these skills and appropriate technologies to communicate and collaborate with parners, colleagues, family, and sometimes the general public.

- Use these skills to actively participate in society and contribute to a informed and committed community.

- Develop tools related to design, technology and robotics.

- Become familiar with free 3d design and print software.

- Participate in fairs and events promoting self-employment, school science and technology projects and innovation.

- Train teachers in work by projects, in the application of them to the classroom and in the development of creativity.

It is a territory of great contrasts, with prosperous growing areas and landscapes characteristic of a dry and somewhat cold climate, the consequence of its height and the absence of warm winds by the natural barrier. The basis of its economy is traditionally agrarian activity "with a high added value, although still requires the development of a food industry" and mining, currently in decline after the closure of the mines.

On the other hand, the centrality and dynamism of Guadix are ââincreased with the implementation of one of the main routes in the territory of Andalusia, the A-92. Additionally, the regional integration is also due to the presence of the railway infrastructure, which crosses the territory both north-south and west-east, which allows connections with Granada, AlmerÃ­a, Murcia and even Madrid.

Along with agriculture, metal mining is historically one of the main economic activities of the region, especially in the municipalities that are located in the Marquesado de Zenete. The closure of the Alquife iron fields is one of the main problems to be faced, since it involves the decline of the important extractive activity that is reduced to the exploitation of calcium carbonate. Limonite and hematite are the main ferric minerals that can be obtained in these mines since Roman times and that make this territory the main mining center of all Penibaetic Andalusia. In spite of this, the maximum benefit cannot be obtained, since the manufacturing industry was in the hands of foreign companies, with the only competition being the extraction of the mineral. Nowadays, the open crater of the Alquife mines and the extinct Lanteira silver deposit (which exploded 100 years ago) are already part of the landscape, transforming the metallic wealth into the manna of rural tourism.

From the economic structure described, it follows that the efforts to continue developing the territory would go on to boost the agri-food industry on the one hand and, on the other, tourist activity.

Regarding the history of the region, it is necessary to emphasize that it has been populated since prehistoric times thanks to its location on the natural route between the Levant and the Guadalquivir valley and between the coast and the Castilian plateau. Thus, there are remnants that attest to this dating back to the Upper Paleolithic and the metal ages. The Phoenicians and Carthaginians civilizations had also taken advantage of the mineral wealth of the area, but it is during the Roman times that Guadix truly became a strategic point. With the occupation of the Goths, the city went into decline and then achieved great splendor during the Muslim era. Numerous vestiges of this time have been preserved one of them being the name that was given to the city: Guad-Haix which means, river of the life. After this point, Christian monuments were constructed one example being the Guadix Cathedral, which stands on the old mosque and in which you can find three architectural styles: Gothic, Renaissance and Baroque.
